I believe the most impactful engineering happens at the boundary between rigorous research and real-world production constraints — where every microstructural decision carries a cost, a performance trade-off, and an industrial consequence.

I am Venkata Santosh Saran Nagineni, an Additive Manufacturing Engineer and MSc candidate in Metallic Materials Technology at TU Bergakademie Freiberg. Currently completing my master’s thesis at DTU in collaboration with Norsk Hydro ASA, investigating process parameter optimisation and microstructure-property relationships for AM-produced high-performance extrusion dies.

My background spans the full AM value chain — from powder characterisation and DOE-driven parameter development through advanced SEM/XRD/XPS/micro-CT characterisation to fatigue modelling and industrial implementation. I have delivered measurable results at LEGO and FEM Forschungsinstitut, and I am now seeking full-time opportunities across Europe and globally.
